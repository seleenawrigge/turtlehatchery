Turtle categories in Sri Lanka

Sea turtles are a protected species in Sri Lanka. There are 5 main species of sea turtles in Sri Lanka, listed below are the different species that call Sri Lanka home and the places in which they can be seen.

Sea turtles in Sri lanka

Olive Ridley

The Olive Ridley turtle is the smallest of the sea turtles and although rare, can be found in kosgoda beach, Sri Lanka. They are named after their olive-coloured shell and can weigh up to 50 kilograms. The Olive Ridley turtle is a vulnerable species due to their nesting behaviour. Unlike the Green turtle, Olive Ridley turtles nest in large groups known as arribadas. This makes them vulnerable to human activities such as beachfront development and egg poaching.

Green Turtle

Sri Lanka is an important nesting site for Green turtles. The beaches along the southern and eastern coasts of the island, such as Rekawa, Kosgoda, and Pigeon Island, are known for their significant Green turtle populations. These areas provide suitable nesting grounds for the turtles, and conservation efforts are in place to protect their nests and ensure their survival. Visitors to Sri Lanka can often witness the incredible sight of female Green turtles coming ashore to lay their eggs during the nesting season.

Hikkaduwa Beach

Sri Lanka is also home to Hawksbill turtles, especially along its coral reefs. These turtles can be found in various coastal areas, including Pigeon Island and Hikkaduwa Beach. Hawksbill turtles are known for their important role in maintaining the health of coral reefs as they feed on sponges and help control their growth. However, like other regions, Hawksbill turtles in Sri Lanka face threats such as habitat degradation and poaching for their unique shells.

Leatherback Turtle

The Leatherback turtle (Dermochelys coriacea) is the largest of all living turtles and is known for its unique leathery skin instead of a hard shell. While Leatherback turtles have a global distribution, they are a rare species in Sri Lanka and can sometimes be seen in Rekawa beach which is known to host Leatherback turtle nesting sites. However, due to their small population size and low nesting numbers, Leatherback turtles are not as frequently encountered in Sri Lanka compared to other turtle species. Conservation efforts and research initiatives are in place to monitor and protect the nesting sites of Leatherback turtles in Sri Lanka, with a focus on raising awareness and implementing measures for their conservation.
